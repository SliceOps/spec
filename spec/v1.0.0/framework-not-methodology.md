# Why SliceOps is a framework, not a methodology

SliceOps is deliberately described as a **framework**, not a "methodology." The distinction is not cosmetic — it determines what SliceOps asks of you and what it leaves to your judgment.

## The difference

A **methodology** prescribes a *process*: a sequence of steps, ceremonies, and roles you follow. Scrum, Waterfall, and XP are methodologies — they tell you *how* to run the work, day to day.

A **framework** provides *load-bearing structure and guarantees* within which you choose your own methods. It defines what must hold true, not the order in which you do things.

SliceOps is the second kind. It defines a small set of non-negotiable principles (Layer A) and reference patterns (Layer B) that make AI-assisted software **auditable by construction** — and then gets out of the way.

## SliceOps is development-style-agnostic

SliceOps **wraps** the way you already work; it does not replace it. Spec-driven, test-driven, AI-DLC, or your own house style — SliceOps sits around it, adding atomic slices, an audit plane, human-in-the-loop authority, and evidence gates. It composes with authoring tools like GitHub Spec Kit or AWS AI-DLC rather than competing with them.

"Methodology" is the wrong word because it implies a rigid, prescribed dance. SliceOps prescribes **outcomes that must hold** — every change is an atomic slice; every decision is recorded; every merge is gated by evidence; a human holds final authority — and leaves the *how* to you.

## An analogy

A methodology is a *recipe*: do these steps, in this order. A framework is a *well-run kitchen*: mise en place, food-safety rules, labeled stations — within which you cook whatever you want, however you like.

SliceOps is the kitchen, not the recipe.

## In practice

- You adopt SliceOps without abandoning your current process — it adds guarantees, not ceremonies.
- "SliceOps-compliant" means you honor the principles and the relevant reference patterns, not that you followed a prescribed sequence.
- It is **decision-driven and evidence-gated**, and it runs on any text-based AI agent, git, and atomic-slice scoping (P8 — Platform-Agnostic).

> SliceOps is the structure that makes AI-first software engineering auditable. The methods you run inside it are yours.
